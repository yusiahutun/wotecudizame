Casey Kasem's children settle their wrongful death case against his wife

Publish date: 2024-07-08

A years-long legal feud between late radio host Casey Kasem's grown children and his second wife has come to an end after both sides finally agreed to a settlement.

As TMZ first reported on Tuesday, Kasem's three children from his first marriage had filed documents asking a judge to throw their wrongful death lawsuit against their stepmother, Jean Kasem

The dismissal of the complaint was finalized this week and both sides signed off on an undisclosed settlement.

However, Casey's daughter Kerri is said to be unhappy with the outcome of the legal battle, a source told the gossip site.

Kerri Kasem was described as feeling 'distraught' and 'heartbroken' by the settlement, which she reportedly felt was forced on her by her two siblings, Mike and Julie, and their lawyers, TMZ reported.

Kasem, the longtime 'American Top 40' host and the voice of Scooby Doo cartoon character Shaggy, had a form of dementia and a severe bedsore when died at age 82 at a hospital in Gig Harbor, southwest of Seattle in June 2014, leaving behind an estate worth up to $100million.

His children and wife fought over visitation access to him before his death, with Kerri Kasem assuming control of his medical care. 

Jean Kasem, 64, who appeared on Cheers and was married to the announcer for more than 30 years, had moved him to Washington state after checking him out of a Los Angeles-area medical facility where he was receiving round-the-clock care.

Jean has contended her decision to relocate her ailing husband was made to protect his privacy. As a result of the move, she was stripped off control of his medical care in the final days of his life.

Jean, a former actress, was married to Casey for more than 30 years. After the radio host's death in 2014, her three stepchildren accused her of elder abuse  

Prosecutors in Los Angeles County in 2015 declined to charge the wife with elder abuse. 

Jean claimed that Casey's children killed their father for his $100million fortune 

That year, three of Casey Kasem's children and his brother filed the wrongful death lawsuit, accusing Jean of elder abuse and inflicting emotional distress on them by restricting access to their father before his death.

'Jean killed my father, what she did led to his death,' Kerri told the true-crime TV show 48 Hours in July. 'The only thing she ever wanted from my dad is money. That's it.' 

Jean counter-sued her stepchildren, accusing them of conspiring to kill their father for money. 

'They killed my husband, they killed their father,' Jean told 48 Hours correspondent Peter Van Sant. 'It was always about the money. We became the bank of Kasem. We were their personal ATM machine.' 

In the fall of 2018, police in Washington state reopened their investigation into Casey's death after Jean submitted a private investigator's report claiming her stepchildren were responsible for the radio host's death.

After a review of the case, police concluded there was no evidence of wrongdoing in Casey's death.
