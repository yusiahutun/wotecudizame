Brian Kowalski's body found off Florida Keys after he jumped in the water to save friend's son

Publish date: 2024-07-02

Father dies after he jumped in the water to save friend's son as his own daughter, 10, looked on in terror

A snorkeling trip off the Florida Keys has ended in a tragedy when a helicopter pilot recovered the body of a man who had drowned while saving a friend's son.

A Florida Fish and Wildlife chopper spotted 39-year-old Brian Kowalski, of Trinity, Wednesday morning floating in the water off Johnson Key two days after he had gone missing.

Kowalski and his 10-year-old daughter, Erin, were boating and snorkeling near West Summerland Key with family friend Steve Weir, his 8-year-old son, Jonathan, and another boy, 7-year-old Caden Wolfe, when the accident happened at around 4pm Monday.

Special bond: Brian Kowalski, pictured here with his daughter, Erin, was found dead in the water off Florida two days after he drowned while saving a little boy

According to Weir, he and Kowalski took turns snorkeling while the two boys swam behind the boat holding onto a trailing rope.

Caden climbed out of the water, but when Jonathan attempted to follow him, the rope got snagged in the propeller, and the current started pulling the 8-year-old away from the boat, Tampa Bay Times reported.

Without a moment’s hesitation, Kowalski jumped in the water in a bid to save Jonathan. Meanwhile, Steve Weir, who was snorkeling at the time, resurfaced and was able to rescue his son.

Jonathan told his father that Brian Kowalski let go of him and was floating face down in the water.    

Weir attempted to turn the boat around and try to save his friend, but the rope coiled around the propeller made the maneuver impossible.

‘I watched him float away the whole time,‘ Erin Kowalski said of her father in an interview with ABC Action News. ‘I was crying and screaming – this is an emergency! Just screaming at the top of my lungs – this is an emergency and we need help now.’

Trip gone awry: Kowalski was snorkeling with his daughter (right), a family friend, his son and another boy when one of the kids got caught in a tide, prompting the 39-year-old man to jump after him 

Desperate search: The U.S. Coast Guard, the Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission and other agencies scoured the waters by air and by sea in search of Kowalski

The U.S. Coast Guard, Sheriff's Office and Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission had searched for Kowalski using boats and helicopters.

The man’s friends and Erin’s mother, Michelle Harper, took to Facebook, urging people in the area who own boats to join the search. 

Closure: Sherry Ross (left), Erin's godmother, confirmed that the family was notified that his mother had been found

Harper posted a number of heart-wrenching messages expressing her desperation and agony over the fate of her daughter's beloved father.

'Erin has to have closure, and not bringing him home in one way or another would be absolutely devastating to us all. PLEASE FIND HIM!!!!!!!! WE CAN'T TAKE THIS!!!!!' she wrote Tuesday.

Just hours before Brian's body was discovered, Michelle Harper wrote about her despair as she kept thinking about her daughter, who was terrified at the prospect of losing her ‘daddy.’

‘How do I tell my beautiful little girl that her daddy may not be coming back, and that the last time she will ever see him is when she was reaching for him and screaming "daddy" as he floated away? HOW?’ she wondered.

Michelle concluded her lengthy post by appealing directly to Brian and begging him to come home for the sake of his heartbroken daughter.

'She needs you, Brian,' she wrote. 'She needs you to come home and wrap your arms around her and tell her how much you love her.

'I am doing everything I can to get you home. Please know that I am not giving up on you and I will continue to do everything that I can for you.'

At around 10.30am Wednesday, an FWC pilot spotted the man's body in the water and brought him ashore.

Happier times: Brian Kowalski, left, pictured in this undated photo with his daughter, Erin, and her mother, Michelle Harper, who on Wednesday eulogized him in a touching Facebook post

Traumatic moment: Erin (left) told reporters how she watched her father float away from the boat as she screamed for help

‘I'm sad and relieved at the same time,’ Weir said Wednesday. ‘I'm glad they actually found him. I'm glad that there will be some closure.’

Sherry Ross, Erin’s godmother, confirmed that the family had been notified. A medical examiner will perform an autopsy to determine Kowalski’s cause of death.

According to the man’s friend David Gentry, the 39-year-old was always ready to help a person in need. 

Gentry told Bay News 9 that in 2003, Kowalski saved a toddler who almost drowned in a bathtub by performing CPR on the boy.
