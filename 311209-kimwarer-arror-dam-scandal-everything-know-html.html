Kimwarer and Arror dam scandal: Everything you need to know

Publish date: 2024-04-10

Kimwarer and Arror dam scandal has been in the headlines for almost a year now. Investigations from responsible authorities have since revealed surprising details. So, what happened over time and how much money did the country lose from the two dams corruption case?

As of today, hundreds of Kimwarer and Arror dam suspects from various government departments have been implicated in the multi-billion scandal. Some Cabinet Secretaries and Permanent Secretaries were also not left out in the case. Below is everything you need to know about Kimwarer and Arror dam scandal in Kenya.

Kimwarer and Arror dam locations

The two projects are located in Elgeyo Marakwet County. Arror multi-purpose dam was to be constructed in Kapsaya, Marakwet West. Kimwarer, on the other hand, is in Talal.

Based on initial reports from the Director of Public Prosecutions, a total of Ksh 19.7 billion had been lost as of January this year. It was about 30% of the total estimated project costs. The amount was used to pay contractors an advance payment before the project could commence.

Noordin Haji indicated that there was no evidence that the land where the project was to take place had been acquired by the government. He further cited that this was a “well-choreographed scheme” by top government officials in collision with private institutions and individuals.

A case file seen by Citizen TV revealed how the money was shared among the involved individuals and companies for supplying goods and services for the project. According to the source, Ksh. 21 billion was lost to commodities never endowed as follows:

  • New Itailico Ltd received about Ksh. 15 million to supply bed sheets, pillows, duvets, towels, and other beddings. Up to today, it is not clear why so much bedding would be ordered in a construction site.
  • Long Rock Engineering Limited was named in the scandal for being paid Ksh. 6.2 million to supply furniture and transport services.
  • Achillies Material Handing Limited was paid Ksh. 6 million to make sure generators are adequate during the exercise.
  • Tusker Mattresses Ltd was allegedly given Ksh. 10.2 million for foodstuff supplies even before the construction had started.
  • Azimuth Group limited pocketed Sh. 18.3 million for aerial survey and engineering services.
  • Express Travel Company is alleged to have been paid Ksh. 14 million to supply air travel tickets.
  • FCMT – A Charleston Travel Limited is said to have received Ksh. 77 million for transport services.
  • Still, on travel services, Toyota Kenya was paid Ksh. 77 million to supply motor vehicles.
  • According to DCI, CMC Di Ravenna Kenya was also on this list for receiving Ksh. 100.6 million for unknown services.
  • Files Steel Structures Limited was given Ksh. 8 million to supply construction material.
  • Sanlam Insurance Company is said to have amassed Ksh. 47 million for cover services.
  • Ksh. 19.4 million went to Highland Valuers for relocation and valuation services even though the land where the projects were to take place is still occupied.
  • Vipul Kumar is alleged to have been given Ksh. 4.7 million for services that are yet to be established by the detectives.

Daily Nation added a few experts to show how taxpayer’s money was illegally and unlawfully paid out. The source stated that the following plotted ways were used to defraud the dams funds:

  • The National Environmental Management Authority (NEMA) gave a nod to the Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA). The activity provided a go-ahead to the two projects. Surprisingly, there is no evidence that the exercise was carried out. Additionally, the body ignored protests from other agencies not to approve Kimwarer and Arror dams initiative.
  • The National Treasury released Ksh 643 million to compensate and resettle people in the areas affected by the move. There is no evidence that there the land was acquired by the government.
  • Kenya Forest Service insisted that excision of forest land to build dams was not a good idea. However, despite that, top officials continued with the projects plans.
  • The Kerio Valley Development Authority (KVDA) circumvented the tendering process to ensure CMC di Ravenna Company was awarded a contract. To do so, officials in authority used the 2005’s Public Procurement and Disposal Act for concession.
  • CMC di Ravenna was awarded a contract while clearly, it was facing a voluntary liquidation in Italy.
  • It is alleged that KVDA didn’t allow competition from suppliers for all the tenders.
  • It was stated that all the contracts would cost Ksh. 46 billion. However, the National Treasury negotiated an increase in the loan to 63 billion shillings. It means that there was an additional Ksh. 17 billion.
  • CMC di Ravenna is said to have submitted technical design drafts on February 2019. It was four years behind the scheduled time.
  • The Kenya National Treasury entered into a loan contract in Euros. This is even though commercial agreements are supposed to be in US dollars, thus causing losses in exchange rates.
  • Ksh. 11 billion is alleged to be paid as upfront for insurance, yet the government guarantee would have at no cost sufficed.
  • Another Sh. 4.6 billion was borrowed by the authorities in addition to the other loan to settle the interest in advance. It means that a loan was acquired and later another added to pay the benefit of the first debt.
  • Borrowed money went direct to personal bank accounts an indication that no one was overseeing their use.

DCI interrogates top leaders

Following the scandal revelation, the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) summoned over 100 directors to his offices for questioning. Henry Rotich was also among the questioned people over the Ksh. 63 billion Arror and Kimwarer multi-purpose dams.

Speaking to the members of the press, Kinoti said that all the directors from the mentioned companies were to provide invoices, quotations, delivery notes, and other related documents.

The companies listed include Silent Night Ltd, Red Court Hotel Company Ltd, Azimuth, Charleston Travel, Oshwal Wholesalers, Infinity Furniture, New Italycor Ltd, Express Travel Group, Amin Automotive Supplies, Adrian-Tricom Kenya Ltd, Steel Structures Ltd, Highlands Valuers Limited among others.

Cabinet Secretaries (CSs) Mwangi Kiunjuri of Agriculture, Eugene Wamalwa of Devolution and Simon Chelugui (Water) together with their Principal Secretaries were also questioned over the matter.

A team of investigators is dispatched to Italy

Haji termed this scandal as one of the biggest in history that saw the country enlist help from Dubai, Italy, and the United Kingdom. He revealed that if the exercise were done according to the law, then it wouldn’t have cost that much. He stated that his office had the evidence proving that Ksh. 19,714,366,911 was lost through a well-planned scheme. For this reason, a team of top security officials was sent in Italy in a bid to recover the money paid by the treasury to the two dams.

DPP Noordin Haji, DCI director George Kinoti and several other selected State Law officials landed in Italy with evidence of fraud to convince their counterparts. The team met Italian Government-owned insurer Service Assicurativi Del Comercio Estero (SACE). It is alleged that the company was paid Ksh 11.1 billion as an insurance premium for the loan acquired to build the dams.

The team was also armed with CCTV footages of the meetings held between Kenyan and Italian officials to negotiate the loan deal.

Kimwarer and Arror dam scandal politicized

The revelation of this corruption case elicited a lot of debate in the country with DP Ruto and Raila engaging in a war of words.

In a personal statement to the senate, Kipchumba Murkomen cited that the DCI was wasting time on investigating the scandal as no money had been lost. The Senator added on and said that the scandal was a weaponized tool for 2022 elections. On the same day, Deputy President William Ruto came out to rubbish the reports that Ksh. 21 billion was lost in Kimwarer and Arror dam projects.

KVDA breaks up silence on the scandal

In a full-page advert on February 28, KDVA broke its silence concerning the embezzlement allegations. The agency stated that the projects were on schedule and they would be completed within the scheduled period.

It came three days after 107 directors were summoned by authorities. KVDA termed the much-hyped news outlets stories as outrageous and misleading. As for the advance payments made, KVDA stated that all the contracts stipulated a down payment of 15% of the total contract sum. The amount would be used to facilitate designs and mobilization.

Kimwarer and Arror dam closes

Investigations into the Ksh. 21 billion probe has now come to an end. The case files were received by the DPP on July 23, 2019.

Speaking to the Sunday Standard, Haji said that the files would be sent back to the DCI for further statement recording. However, he declined to discuss details of the investigations citing that the record of statements was not yet complete. The file was received by ODPP the same day, an indication that high-level arrests were to take place.

According to the Sunday Standard, there were doubts about the possibility of arrests. Claims had it that the scandal had been frozen by the State House for political reasons as arrests and prosecution of top officials could split Uhuru’s government.

Suspects face 24 charges in court

National Treasury CS Henry Rotich and other officials were arrested in the multi-billion Kimwarer and Arror dam scandals. Both of them face 24 charges among them fraud conspiracy, abuse of office and engaging in the projects without proper planning.

All the 26 suspects denied the allegations pleading not guilty before Chief Magistrate, Douglas Ogoti and the prosecution team which was led by Taib Ali Taib, Emily Kamau, Alexander Muteti and Victor Owiti.

On the other hand, the defence team comprised of Kioko Kilukumi, Katwa Kigen, Kipchumba Murkomen, Philip Nyachoti, Stephen Kimathi, Jinaro Kibet, Mahat Samene, and Kelvin Anami Mwai Mugo.

On July 23, CS Rotich and PS Kamau Thugge were released on a Ksh. 50 million bail with a surety of a similar amount. Also released was Permanent Secretary Susan Koech who was granted a Ksh. 6 million bail and an option of Ksh. 30 million bond.

All the tender committee members were also released together with NEMA general director Geoffrey Wakhungu on Ksh. 750,000 bail.

The Kimwarer and Arror dam scandal is undoubtedly the biggest probe ever witnessed in the country for the last two years. We can only hope that the nine months DCI and DPP investigations and the ongoing trial will solve the mystery.
