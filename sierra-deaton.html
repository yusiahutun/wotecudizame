Sierra Deaton Biography, Age, Height, Boyfriend, Net Worth

Publish date: 2024-07-25

Sierra Deaton is an American singer and social media influencer. She caught everyone’s eyes when she won on the stage The X factor US’ final season in the year 2013 with Alex. They were the half-pop group. They were the first and only group to win the American version of the show, and they did so on the final episode before it was canceled.

Early life

Sierra Deaton was born on February 11, 1991, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Sierra comes from a multi-ethnic family; her father, Dr. John Deaton, is British whereas her mother, Vu Deaton, is Vietnamese. She also has an older sister named Lara, and the two grew up in Orlando, Florida, with their family. According to The Famous People, she began dancing at the age of two and is proficient in Ballet, Jazz, Hip-Hop, and Contemporary. She also participated in Irish Dancing 7 times and won 3 titles. Sierra attended Lake Howell High School for her high schooling. She attended The University of Central Florida located in Florida for her graduation.

Sierra Deaton Career

Deaton started writing music with the nickname of Essy. Sierra Deaton and her ex-boyfriend, musician Alex Kinsey, began performing together after Alex discovered her talent while they were dating. They auditioned for The X Factor US in New Orleans, performing a sultry cover of Britney Spears’ Toxic. They wowed the judges with their performance and made it to the top 40. On the show, they were mentored by music critic Simon Cowell. They gave their first live performance on the show after reaching the top 12 with a stripped-down version of Robin Thicke’s Blurred Lines. In December 2013, Alex and Sierra have crowned X Factor US winners, becoming the only duo from the American version of the show and the fourth in the franchise to do so.

Sierra and Alex announced the title and cover of their debut album It’s About Us in July 2014, and four days later, they released their promotional single Little Do You Know. After breaking up in September 2017 and deciding to part ways, the duo stopped making music together.
