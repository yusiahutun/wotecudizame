They are both award-winning musicians celebrated for their singing talent. 

But Kellie Pickler, 26, and Sheryl Crow, 51, ensured the attention was on something else entirely on Wednesday night.

The singers both showed off major cleavage in very low-cut gowns at the Country Music Television Awards in Nashville, Tennessee.

Flaunting their assets: Sheryl Crow and Kellie Pickler showed off major cleavage in low-cut gowns at the CMT Awards in Nashville, Tennessee on Wednesday night

Kellie looked sensational in a glittering pink gown that barely covered her sizable assets.

The blonde - who recently won Dancing With The Stars - showed off her toned arms and slender figure in the form-fitting frock.

She wore her signature short hair combed back, and added a slick of rose-coloured lipgloss to complement her dress.

Glittering gal: Kellie showed off her toned arms and slender figure in the form-fitting number as she smiled for the cameras

The champion's arrived: Kellie, who recently won Dancing With The Stars, showed off her slender waist in the form-fitting gown

Sheryl, meanwhile, showcased her incredibly toned arms in the strapless white Grecian dress.

The front of the gown sported cutouts on the bust that left little to the imagination.

She paired the frock with platform gold heels and wore her blonde hair in a stylish updo.

Golden girl: Sheryl wore gold platform heels and accessorised with a gold clutch while wearing her blond hair in a stylish updo

A legend: Sheryl presented the award for video of the year alongside Kenny Rogers, who looked smart in a waistcoat

Sheryl recently opened up about her past relationship with ex-fiance Lance Armstrong, and what she may have known during her relationship with the champion cyclist who recently admitted to doping.

'The only thing I would say about Lance is that his story is completely his own and has nothing to do with my life, and so I don’t speak about it,' Sheryld told the Associated Press in May.

'I was asked a question on a TV show once, which caught me off-guard, and I feel like I kind of answered it, and that’s all I can say about it. It really has zero relevance, and I’m sorry that people still want to attach me to that, but it holds no relevance to my life.'
