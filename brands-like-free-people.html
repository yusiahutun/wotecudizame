Affordable, Boho Clothing to Shop in 2024

If you ask any fashionista or shopaholic for the pinnacle of bohemian fashion, you’re bound to hear about Free People. No, actually, I dare you to find a brand or retailer that does boho style better than FP. It’s truly in a league of its own, but if you’re looking to branch out, there’s a handful of brands like Free People that deserve your attention.

Let’s start with the fact that this retailer has undergone a pretty noticeable transformation since its lacy bralette and flowy dress days in the early 2000s. Sure, those signature pieces are the bread and butter of Free People, but it’s since embraced more modern silhouettes, designs, and trends (I spotted Y2K-, sporty-, and coquette-coded pieces). It has even expanded to beauty and activewear (think FP Movement). Bottom line: I keep getting served Free People in my Instagram feed, and it’s convinced me that I need to not only return to the retailer’s site and load up my cart, but also find some alternatives.

Where can you find brands like Free People? I’d start with Urban Outfitters, Revolve, and Anthropologie. They all carry garments that embody the laidback, casual, trendy FP vibe, but they also offer a ton of other aesthetics so you can truly find a little bit of everything on these sites. Plus, our top picks come at affordable prices, so you won’t break the bank while trying to nail the FP vibe.

Below, find five brands like Free People that can help update your spring wardrobe with affordable, boho, trendy closet staples.

  • Urban Outfitters

    Urban Outfitters is without a doubt the closest thing you’ll find to Free People. Like FP, UO gathers a multitude of in-house and independent brands into one convenient shopping destination. You won’t have to dig much before spotting a ton of Free People-esque styles—from these flowy pants to this breezy dress. Free People is also known for its variety of basics, and I can’t help but see similarities between FP and Urban Outfitters’ intimate loungewear line, Out From Under.

  • Revolve

    While Revolve might not be the first thing that comes to mind when thinking of Free People alternatives, trust me, it makes sense. The former features high-quality denim, bold patterns, intricate textures, and vibrant colors in spades. Revolve’s entire site feels like you’re on vacation along the coast or singing your heart out at a festival, and don’t these sound like occasions you’d rely on a retailer like Free People for?

  • Anthropologie

    Name a retailer that does prints and textures better than Anthropologie—I’ll wait. If you’ve ever browsed an Anthro store in person, you’ll know it’s impossible to resist touching all of the beautifully made garments hanging from the racks. Free People carries a decent amount of garments with floral patterns, as well as unique textures like patchwork details (as shown in the jeans below), quilted pieces, and more. You can easily find near-identical designs at Anthropologie, if not on an even more dramatic scale. The only catch is that Anthro leans on the pricier end of the spectrum, but check out the sale section for absolute gems.

  • Tularosa

    Tularosa considers romantic dresses, textural jackets, and embroidered tops its specialties—and all with a vintage-inspired flair. Sound familiar? I’d say that’s pretty close to what you can find at Free People, especially when it comes to romantic dresses and more feminine pieces. Speaking of texture, can we please have a moment for this crochet maxi cardigan? I might just be a newly converted fan.

  • For Love & Lemons

    If you’re trying to match Free People’s softer and more dainty side, look no further than For Love & Lemons. The shorts below remind me of the bloomers I recently spotted on FP’s site. Plus, the black corset top is reminiscent of Free People’s never-ending selection of lacy bras and bralettes. There’s no shortage of playful colors, poofy sleeves, ruffles, and flowy silhouettes at For Love & Lemons.
