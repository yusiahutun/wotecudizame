Newlyweds Ben and Caroline Davies found safe

A newlywed British couple who went missing for three weeks after going camping in the Canadian mountains on their honeymoon have been found safe and well.

Ben and Caroline Davies had not been heard from since June 22 when they headed into the wilderness and when they were found they said they had no idea that they had been reported missing.

The adventurous couple were exploring the mountains and went kayaking in a remote area of British Columbia that is known for its high numbers of grizzly bears.

Ben and Caroline Davies turned up safe and well, unaware of the search for them

Relatives raised the alarm when Mr Davies, 37, and his 28-year-old wife, from Leintwardine, Herefordshire, failed to catch their flights home on July 4.

The Royal Canadian Mounted Police launched a search for the couple and an officer spotted their black pick-up truck which had been borrowed from a relative.

Sergeant Rob Vermeulen said: 'We thank the public and our community partners like conservation officers and park rangers, that assisted in looking for this couple and passing along information.

'We are pleased the couple have been located safe and this is the outcome we were all hoping for.'

The couple, who left on their four-week honeymoon at the beginning of last month, were last heard from when they were staying in the small town of Smithers, more than 400 miles north of Vancouver and 117 miles from Alaska.

They were described as being 'experienced outdoorsmen' and their lack of contact with their family baffled police.
