High Frequency Hair Treatment: A Modern Solution for Hair Growth

Publish date: 2024-10-05

Hair treatments have come a long way from the conventional oils, serums, and massages of yesteryears. In the contemporary era of hair care, high-frequency hair treatment is making waves. This trailblazing therapy is fast becoming the preferred choice for individuals grappling with hair loss, dandruff, and other hair-related challenges. Its appeal? It's not just any treatment; it's an avant-garde method of revitalizing hair from its roots.

The magic of this treatment is its potent use of UV and high-frequency rays, directly addressing hair follicles (1). This dual action not only boosts blood circulation but also infuses the hair roots with essential nutrients, leading to robust and radiant hair growth. It seamlessly merges the realms of science and aesthetics, offering an unparalleled hair care experience.

As the hair care domain continually advances, it presents innovative methods that seek to nourish and protect our cherished locks. Among these innovations, high-frequency hair treatment emerges as a standout, promising a transformative hair journey.

What Is High Frequency Hair Treatment?

High-frequency hair treatment is a modern therapeutic solution for hair thinning, excessive hair loss, and uncontrolled dandruff. Along with the ultraviolet and high-frequency rays used in the procedure to invigorate hair follicles, this treatment also needs a high-frequency wand for hair growth (2). It's a rod-like tool that, upon touching the scalp, emits electrical stimulation. This action revitalizes the hair follicles, fostering healthier and thicker hair growth. The treatment's essence is its capacity to augment the blood flow to hair follicles, ensuring they are adequately nourished.

How Does High Frequency Hair Treatment Work?

At its core, high frequency treatment for hair loss leverages the power of electricity to rejuvenate and stimulate hair growth. The electrical stimulation may have the potential to trigger hair growth (3). But, like many groundbreaking techniques, the complete understanding of its efficacy, especially in humans, still requires more in-depth research.

The treatment stands on three foundational pillars that contribute to its hair growth-promoting capabilities:

  • Follicle stimulation: By directly acting on the hair follicles, it encourages them to grow hair at an accelerated pace.
  • Enhanced blood circulation: A healthy blood flow is crucial for any organ, and our scalp is no exception (4). By improving blood circulation to the scalp, the treatment ensures that essential nutrients find their way to the hair roots, nourishing them and facilitating hair growth.
  • Promotion of cellular health: A faster cell turnover rate is beneficial for scalp health (5). This means old cells slough off more rapidly, making way for new, healthy ones. Such a cycle reduces dandruff, keeps the scalp in prime health, and ensures the hair remains vibrant and robust.

    While we await more comprehensive research to endorse its benefits fully, the initial results and understanding offer a ray of hope for many seeking a remedy for their hair woes. As always, the key lies in understanding, embracing, and innovating.

    High-Frequency Hair Treatment Procedure And Duration

    High-frequency hair treatment, a modern solution to many hair woes, comes with its unique procedure. If you're considering getting this treatment, understanding its step-by-step process and the time it demands is crucial.

    The Procedure:

    • Sectioning the Hair: The professional divides the hair into 5-6 manageable sections to ensure thorough treatment.
    • Comb Treatment: A specialized comb is maneuvered over the affected areas for 2-3 minutes per section.
    • Massage: A revitalizing hair massage follows, lasting for about 20-25 minutes, stimulating the scalp and promoting relaxation (4).
    • Applying Conditioning Mask: A nourishing conditioning mask is then applied meticulously from the roots to the tips of the hair.
    • Steaming and UV Exposure: The hair undergoes steaming for about 10 minutes, followed by exposure to UV bulb rays for another 7-8 minutes.

    Post-treatment Care:

    It's recommended that the hair be washed the day following the treatment to ensure the scalp remains clean and free from any product residue.

    How Often Can You Use High Frequency for Hair Growth?

    Typically, physicians recommend undergoing 10-12 consistent sessions, with treatments scheduled two to three times a week. Based on the outcomes, the frequency of these sessions might be adjusted to once or twice weekly, eventually tapering down to once every two weeks. Continuing the treatment is essential to ensure sustained hair nourishment, restoration, and overall hair well-being.

    Benefits of High Frequency Hair Treatment

    High-frequency hair treatment is gaining rapid popularity. Numerous benefits are there which you must know before taking up high frequency hair treatment. Let’s take a look.
     

    • Neutralizing scalp pH: High-frequency treatment effectively creates a neutral pH value in the head region, which leads to improved secretions and a balanced scalp environment (5). 
    • Dandruff and itchy scalp protection: The treatment stimulates the sebaceous glands to produce sebum (6). This natural substance not only moisturizes the scalp but also eliminates the chances of getting fungal infections. This double action significantly reduces dandruff and scalp itchiness, often culprits for hair loss.
    • Improved scalp metabolism and blood circulation: Electric therapy enhances the scalp's metabolism (7). Simultaneously, blood circulation in the head region gets a boost, offering soothing effects both physically and mentally (5).
    • Healthy hair growth: The treatment actively promotes the growth of robust and healthy hairs, resulting in reduced hair loss. It ensures improved nutrient supply to the hair bulb region.
    • Combating hair thinning: By preventing dead skin cell accumulation and ensuring even oil distribution across the scalp, the treatment revitalizes hair follicles. This makes the hair strands stronger and less prone to thinning (6).
    • Promoting relaxation: The repeated head massages and hair steams during the procedure offer relaxation (4). Additionally, it's worth noting that high-frequency treatments are appropriate for adult patients.

    The benefits of high-frequency hair treatment are manifold. From tackling dandruff to promoting relaxation, it's a comprehensive solution for various hair concerns.

    Side Effects of High Frequency Hair Treatment

    While using high-frequency for hair growth offers numerous advantages, like all treatments, it can have its cons. It's essential to be aware of potential side effects.

    • Hair dryness: One might notice that their hair feels unusually dry after undergoing the treatment.
    • Overactive sebaceous glands: In some cases, the sebaceous glands might produce excess sebum, leading to a greasy scalp.
    • Heat marks and scarring: The heat generated by the high-frequency machine for hair might occasionally cause dark marks or scars on the treated scalp area.

    As soon as you have proper knowledge of these side effects, you can make a well-considered decision and ensure post-treatment care if you wish to proceed with the treatment.
