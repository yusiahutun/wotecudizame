Matthew Perry's final texts he sent to his co-star is a tear-jerker

Publish date: 2024-10-04

As news broke of the tragic death of Matthew Perry, fans and celebrities around the world are paying tribute to the beloved star, including his co-stars over the many years.

Although Perry rose to fame with his iconic role as Chandler Bing on the TV show, Friends, he had many acting credits in other shows and movies before, and after.

Matthew Perry’s co-star Ione Skye shares tear-jerking text message

Ione Skye, who starred alongside Matthew Perry in the film, A Night in the Life of Jimmy Reardon in 1988, has taken to Instagram to pay tribute, as well as share her final text message from the star.

The Hollywood actress, who starred alongside Matt in his first film, shared a photo of the two in the early days, as she penned:

“My last exchange a week ago. I’m very very sad. Loved this guy.”

In the second slide, she shared a screenshot of her last message from Perry, which read: “Hi! I hope all is well. I was meditating (I meditate now) and ‘In Your Eyes’ started playing. And I instantly thought of how beautiful you are,” which is in reference to the song from her 1989 film Say Anything.

To which, Ione responded: “Awe. I love that. Xo.” Perry then said: “Hope you are healthy and happy.”

It seemed like it was the first time the pair had spoken for a while, as Skye wrote back: “Yes, I am both. I think! Good to hear from you. I only have nice memories with you.”

Matthew then agreed before responding: That one afternoon, I was just sitting in my apartment, and there you were!”

Celebrities and friends pay tribute to the late star

Since his tragic passing, at age 54, many celebrities and endless numbers of fans have paid tribute to the star.

Maggie Wheeler, who played the character of Janice, Chandler’s on-again-off-again girlfriend in the early seasons, posted a throwback tribute photo of the two on Instagram.

Music stars including Adele and Charlie Puth also dedicated parts of their concert to remembering the star.

Friends co-creators Marta Kauffman and David Crane, and executive producer Kevin Bright, shared a joint statement on Sunday, in which they said: “This truly is The One Where Our Hearts Are Broken.”

“We are shocked and deeply, deeply saddened by our beloved friend Matthew’s passing,” Kauffman, Crane and Bright wrote. “We will always cherish the joy, the light, the blinding intelligence he brought to every moment – not just to his work, but in life as well.

He was always the funniest person in the room. More than that, he was the sweetest, with a giving and selfless heart.”
