The Veronicas are proud they 'didn't get slaughtered' for posing naked

Publish date: 2024-07-13

After previously being the victims of online 'bodyshamers', The Veronicas are thrilled that their new single cover has been more positively received by fans. 

The 31-year-old Australian twins, Lisa and Jessica Origliasso, pose completely naked on the racy cover for next single In My Blood, with their entire bodies simply smothered with glittery purple paint.

Speaking to News Corp, Jessica said: 'The response has been really beautiful; there hasn’t been any "You are too skinny"'.

'We have boobs and hips, we are women,' she continued. 'We are 31 now and I have grown an arse. Your body changes in your 30s and I am really proud we didn’t get slaughtered in that capacity.' 

The In My Blood cover, which was released earlier this month, sees the singing sensations leaving very little to the imagination with sparkling magenta paint applied from their toes up to the top of their necks.

Looking out into the distance, presumably deep in thought, the brunette beauties sport striking scarlet pouts and their signature bold black winged eyeliner.

Speaking to KIIS 106.5 FM's Kyle and Jackie O show on Friday morning, the girls explained that there was plenty of glitter lingering after the shoot. 

Proud: 'Your body changes in your 30s and I am really proud we didn’t get slaughtered in that capacity,' Jess Origliasso told News Corp - the singer pictured with her twin sister Lisa in a sneak peek video 

'I peed glitter for two or three days after,' one of them joked, before revealing 'we had strategically placed tape' to just cover their modesty. 

In an official press release, the singing duo remained rather coy on the reason behind baring all for the daring cover, which was shot in Los Angeles by photographer Sasha Samsonova.

'It's that elated, euphoric feeling of love at first sight,' the girls said, reiterating the message in their Instagram post. 

They added: 'Love and adrenalin are rushing through your blood. It's like a chemical injection. You've instantly connected with someone. You're knocked out by this person'. 

Last year the twins hit back at critics deeming their slender frames as too 'skinny'.

Out of this world: Jessica and Lisa said their look is inspired by X-Men character Mystique

Celebrating their figure: The singers are proud to embrace their changing bodies  

Speaking to The Daily Telegraph in July last year, Lisa said: 'We have never been more healthy, more sexy, we feel good in our own bodies'.

Explaining she and Jessica are genetically petite, the ARIA award winning artist said she is most concerned about people's perceptions that the famous siblings are 'unhealthy' - a battle with trolls that the pair can't seem to win.

'If you’re skinny,' she said. 'They say you’re unhealthy. If you’re fat, you’re unhealthy. Health is not a weight. There is no weight that equals a clean bill of health.' 

In My Blood is released on June 10, with the starlets having released their last studio album, a self-titled effort, in 2014. 

Quirky: The twins said Australian singer Sia's bold headpieces inspired their choice of green wigs 

It was a huge success in Australia, reaching platinum sales and spawning the No. 1 hit, You Ruined Me.

Unfortunately, the album failed to make a dent in America, where the pair had previously done well thanks to their bubblegum hit Untouched.

After the disc missed out on the Billboard 200 chart altogether, the sisters cancelled their scheduled U.S. tour, citing Visa issues.

The Veronicas' last single was Cruel, which peaked at No. 53 in Australia.
