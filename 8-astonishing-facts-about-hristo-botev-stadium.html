The Home of Bulgarian Football

Hristo Botev Stadium, located in the heart of Sofia, is the largest sports venue in Bulgaria and serves as the national stadium for football matches. With a capacity of over 43,000, it has witnessed countless moments of triumph and glory. Let’s explore some astonishing facts about this iconic stadium.

A Modern Architectural Marvel

One of the most remarkable aspects of Hristo Botev Stadium is its unique architectural design. The stadium, which was built in 1967, features a distinctive oval shape, with each stand seamlessly blending into the next. This creates a visually stunning and harmonious environment for spectators to enjoy the game.

A Historical Landmark

Hristo Botev Stadium holds a significant place in Bulgarian history. It was named after the revolutionary poet Hristo Botev, who played a vital role in the country’s struggle for independence. The stadium stands as a symbol of national pride, honoring the legacy of a national hero.
