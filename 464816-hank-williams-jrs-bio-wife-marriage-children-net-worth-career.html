Hank Williams Jr.'s bio: wife, marriage, children, net worth, career

Publish date: 2024-10-09

Hank Williams Jr. is a country musician known for his unconventional musical style and uncanny talent. When his father, Hank Williams Sr., died, one would have thought his shoes were too big for his son to fill. Big they were, but the younger Williams exceeded the expectations of many to emerge from his father's shadow and introduced a unique sound that had never been heard in country and southern rock music. He is still an icon in country music several decades after his breakthrough in the 1970s.

Hank Williams Jr. became so great partly because he had the time to experiment and rediscover himself as a musician. He was 8 years old when he started performing, but at that time, he was copying his late father's style.

After learning from some of the genre's greats like Jerry Lee Lewis and Earl Scruggs, he realized that he needed a change in musical direction to leave a legacy of his own, and that is what he did.

Early life

The singer was born as Randall Hank Williams on the 26th of May, 1949, in Shreveport, Louisiana, to country musician Hank Williams Sr. and his wife, Audrey Williams. His father gave him the nickname Bocephus after Grand Ole Opry comedian Rod Brasfield's ventriloquist dummy.

Hank Williams Sr. died in 1953 when his son was 3 years old, leaving him to be raised by his mother. Williams has a half-brother named Jett Williams. During his childhood, several contemporary musicians, including Jerry Lee Lewis, Merle Haggard, Fats Domino, Johnny Cash, Earl Scruggs, and Lightnin' Hopkins, frequented his home and taught him various music instruments and styles.

He was a student at the John Overton High School in Nashville, Tennessee. While there, he participated in music events, including taking his guitar to music class and playing for pep rallies. He was also active in the performances of the choir.

Career

In his early career days, from around the age of 8, Williams followed in his famed father's footsteps and used to cover his father's songs and imitated his style. He toured with his mother performing his father's biggest hits and made his first appearance on the Grand Ole Opry when he was 11.

His first television appearance was in a 1964 episode of The Jimmy Dean Show on ABC, where he sang his father's songs. Although his recordings of his father's songs had earned him a reputation as the "Hank Williams impersonator," Williams became disillusioned and cut ties with his mother.

He started recording songs different from his father's, but he had also gotten into drug and alcohol abuse and attempted sui*cide.

A change in musical direction

After the failed suic*ide attempt, he moved to Alabama and started creating music with southern rock musicians like Toy Caldwell, Charlie Daniels, and Waylon Jennings. The album Hank Williams Jr. and Friends (1975), in which he had collaborated with these musicians, became his breakthrough.

In August 1975, Williams nearly died after a mountain-climbing accident in southwestern Montana. As he was climbing Ajax Peak, the snow beneath him collapsed, and he fell almost 500 feet and suffered multiple skull and facial fractures.

Hank Williams Jr.'s face has several scars from the accident, which he conceals with his dark glasses and a hat. The accident is chronicled in his semi-autobiographical film Living Proof: The Hank Williams Jr. Story. It took him two years to recover, and he once again returned to music, but he first had to learn to talk and sing again.

Williams hit his peak partly thanks to his new voice and remained prolific throughout the 1980s into the 90s. Between 1979 and 1992, he released a total of 21 albums, and all were certified gold by the RIAA.

He is well known for his hit All My Rowdy Friends Are Coming Over Tonight, which became the theme song for Monday Night Football. His opening themes for Monday Night Football in 1991, 1992, 1993, and 1994 earned him four Emmy Awards.

Wife and children

Hank Williams Jr.'s partner was Gwen Yeargain, but they parted ways in 1977. However, they had a son together whom they named Shelton Hank Williams. Shelton is also a country musician who performs using the name Hank III.

Williams' second wife was Becky White. Becky and Williams had two daughters, Holly and Hilary. Both of them are also involved in the family trade. Unfortunately, this marriage did not last just like the first one.

In 1990, Williams married a woman named Mary Jane for the third time. They had two children together, namely, Katherine and Samuel. Katherine, the only one of Hank Williams Jr.'s children who was not involved in music, died in a car crash in 2020.

The marriage had its fair share of challenges, and the couple separated in 2007. However, they later reconciled and remained together until March 2022, when Mary Jane died at 58.
