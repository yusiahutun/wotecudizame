What is a color-depositing conditioner?

We love coloring our hair, but let’s face it: Colored hair requires some maintenance. Whether it’s due to heat styling, sun damage, or simply the passage of time, all hair colors fade. This where the best color-depositing conditioners come in. If you want to keep your chosen hue looking vibrant, a color-depositing conditioner will be your saving grace.

Color-depositing conditioners are hair conditioners that contain a small amount of pigment. This can preserve and amplify the shade of your hair, and also help to neutralize unwanted brassy tones that are often found in blonde and naturally gray hair.

How often should you use color-depositing conditioner?

It’s really up to you how often you want to use a color-depositing conditioner. If you wash your hair every day, you might want to save the best color-depositing conditioners for use just once of twice a week. On the other hand, using it every day is perfectly safe — and if you love the results, why not use it every time?

Whether you use a color-depositing conditioner in place of your regular daily conditioner, or more sparingly, adding one of the best color-depositing conditioners to your routine can make you look like you stepped out of the salon, even when it’s been a while since your last visit.

Can you make your own color-depositing conditioner?

It stands to reason: If the best color-depositing conditioners are simply regular conditioners mixed with a little bit of semi-permanent hair dye, can’t you just make your own? However, it’s not that simple. While it may seem like a tempting and cost-effective DIY, it’s a safe bet that you’ll regret whipping up your own mixture in the long run.

Semi-permanent hair dyes, while safe to use on hair, contain drying alcohols and acids that can throw off the blend of emollient ingredients found in regular conditioners. At best, you’ll end up with a mixture that adds a small amount of pigment to the hair but barely hydrates it. And at worst, you could end up creating a chemically unbalanced concoction that actually damages your hair, leaving it frizzy and dry.

Sticking with the best color-depositing conditioners will help you avoid a failed science experiment and keep your locks looking lovely. And since we’ve rounded them up for you, finding the perfect one for you couldn’t be any easier!

To get the best results, use a conditioner specifically formulated to add color to the hair — their ingredients are designed to work together. Not sure where to start looking?
