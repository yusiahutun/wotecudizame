How was the Career of Judith Chapman?

Judith Chapman made her film debut at the age of 16 in “Sette donne per I MacGregor,” a spaghetti western.
She auditioned for the role of Pam Ewing on the CBS primetime serial opera “Dallas” in 1977.
Since the mid-1970s, she has also appeared in a number of daytime dramas.
From 1975 through 1978, her first soap role was as manipulative Natalie Bannon (one of Tom Hughes’ wives) on “As the World Turns.”
In 1983, she received critical acclaim for her four-month appearance as the intriguing Charlotte Greer on Ryan’s Hope.
Her character was part of a revenge plan against the Ryan family after falsely claiming to be Frank Ryan’s ex-wife to the press.
The next year, she joined the cast of “General Hospital” as the cunning Ginny Blake, the biological mother of Mike Webber, who was adopted by Rick and Lesley.
Rick and Ginny married in an attempt to share custody following Lesley’s “death,” and fell in love after Ginny was discovered to be the murderer of D.L. Brock.
After that, she had a brief appearance as Sandra Montaigne, a con artist out to defraud ex-boyfriend Jonathan Russell on “One Life to Live.”
She was the third former General Hospital actress to play Anjelica Devereaux Curtis on “Days of Our Lives,” appearing from 1989 to 1990 and for a brief period in 1991.
She then made her debut appearance on “The Young and the Restless,” filling in for Jess Walton as Jill Abbott for a few episodes.
In January 2005, she became a full-time cast member of “The Young and the Restless,” replacing Joan Van Ark in the contract role of Gloria Fisher.
In March 2011, it was revealed that she had been released from her contract and was now working on a recurrent basis.
In October 2014, it was revealed that she would guest star on “The Bold and the Beautiful” as Gloria.
In 2015 and 2016, she featured in a few more episodes of “The Young and the Restless.”
In 2019, she was on “Days of Our Lives” for two months before returning to “The Young and the Restless.”
She appeared in the final episode of “Galactica 1980” as a guest star.
She also appeared in three episodes of “Murder, She Wrote” and two episodes of “Magnum, P.I.” In the 2002 romantic comedy “The Sweetest Thing,” she played Judy’s mother, played by Parker Posey.
