'I was tired of hearing your voice': First Aid Kit sibling sensations Johanna and Klara Soderberg say non stop touring with each other has taken it's toll on their relationship

By Laura Evans

It's six years since First Aid Kit's Swedish sisters Johanna and Klara Soderberg burst onto the music scene uploading their life changing YouTube rendition of Fleet Foxes, Tiger Mountain Peasant Song.

Now releasing their third album, Stay Gold, the alt folk singers say that non-stop touring together has put pressure on their relationship.

Speaking to Daily Mail Australia the previously joined-at-the-hip twosome reveal they no longer live together.

Fed up? First Aid Kit's sibling singing sensations Johanna and Klara Soderberg say that constant touring has taken it's toll on their relationship

'It came to a point when we toured the last record [The Lion's Roar] that we were so tired of each other,' said Johanna, 23, who plays the keys.

'It's true - I was just tired of your [Klara] voice.

'We used to [live together], but now we don't. That really helps because when we get on tour we're excited to see each other.'

Bluntness is clearly a family trait as Klara concurred: 'It's true - with anyone if you spend that much time together - we're constantly together.'

Natural talents: Klara (pictured) and Johanna first burst onto the scene six years ago with their YouTube rendition of a Fleet Foxes song

What a pair of lungs! The sisters have had to stop living together for the sake of their music

While older sister Johanna is based in Stockholm, Klara has put down roots in Manchester with her fiance, musician Jo Rose, who was opening the duo's Sydney gig at the Metro last week.

On stage it's clear that the seventies sounding sisters are still very much in unison.

While their father, a former Lolita Pop star, handles the sound behind the scenes, the gold-clad beauties charm the audience with their soulful voices.

Known for their perfectly blended harmonies, the singing sister act perform aching melodies about lost love and fleeting change.

Now touring their Mike Mogis produced third album, which takes its name from Robert Frost's famous poem Nothing Gold Can Stay, the talented sisters say they're happy to be back in Australia for the fourth time.

Owning the stage at Byron Bay's Splendour in the Grass, the Scandinavian twosome said they prioritize coming Down Under because they know what it feels like when bands skip isolated destinations.

Dazzling: The sisters are currently touring their third album, Mike Mogis produced Stay Gold

And while Lily Allen was busy staging faux arrests and wearing provocative outfits, the sisters, who co-write their lyrics, say they're all about the music.

'Everything is in service to the song,' says Klara who says they wanted to work with Mogis once more because they felt they had 'unfinished business' and it was 'a natural continuation'.

'Folk music - it's so sparse that you have to have a really good song.'

And while the accomplished talents have played for the likes of Patti Smith and Paul Simon, as well as recording with Jack White, they still say women struggle to secure headline spots at festivals.

'I think it's just harder for women in general whatever you do - to be taken seriously and in what we do - we'd love to see more women,' says Klara.

'Sometimes we're the only female band in the whole festival all the headline acts are male,' chimes in Johanna.

Musical lovers: Klara is engaged to musician Jo Rose (pictured) he's supporting the sisters on their current tour

The sisters say that there is pressure for female artists, such as Miley Cyrus, to resort to risque onstage outfits to garner attention.

'I think it's sad that women have to do that [wear risque outfits], but men can just go up in a T-shirt and shorts,' says Johanna.

'For girls it is all about image.

'I like putting on fancy dresses and stuff, but its just so much focus and comment and you're always judged by that and I wish it would stop.

'It doesn't really help when Miley does stuff like that for women to be taken seriously.'
