Lawyer says he expects charges to be pursued for Alabama cop Dana Elmore who used a stun gun on a Mi

Publish date: 2024-04-05

The lawyer of an handcuffed Alabama man who was tasered by an officer as she told him to 'shut you b**** a** up' said he expects criminal charges to be pursued for the suspended cop.

Leroy Maxwell Jr., the attorney defending Micah Washington, 24, said the investigation into the viral incident remains ongoing. Video captured Reform police officer Dana Elmore's actions during the December 2 drug arrest drawing strong outrage. 

'We will not stop relentlessly pursuing justice for Washington until every responsible party is held accountable for the excessive use of force and false charges made against him,' Maxwell said in a press release. 

Washington has since been released from jail and the drug trafficking and gun possession charges have been dismissed, though other charges remain. 

'We expect the remain charges to be dismissed as well,' Washington's lawyer said. 

Elmore was placed on leave as the investigation unfolds.  

Leroy Maxwell Jr., the attorney defending Micah Washington (pictured), said that he expects criminal charges against officer Dana Elmore to be pursued

On December 2, Reform police officer Dana Elmore was filmed using a stun gun on handcuffed Washington and telling him to 'shut his b**** a*** up'

Elmore has been placed on leave after footage of her response was widely criticized

In a recent interview with ABC, Washington revealed the incident began when he and his brother were changing a flat tire on the side of County Road 27 near his aunt's home when Elmore approached and asked to see his ID.

Washington then asked what was wrong and why he had to show his ID, as he complied with her order.

'I was respectful saying yes ma'am, no ma'am,' he said. 

'She asked me to go stand in the back of the car but I was like "I'm not being detained and I don't have to do anything".'

'She grabbed me and I was standing between the back door and driver door, trying to turn my camera on and record her but she tased me for the first time and put her foot in my back after I fell.'

When Washington fell on the hood of the car, his brother, Shikeem Washington, began to record the video. 

After Elmore ordered the suspect to get up, Washington says he walked to the car in handcuffs and told her that he had a gun in his pocket, which he was legally carrying.

The laughing officer grabbed the gun that was in his pocket before saying, 'oh yeah,' and deployed the stun gun once again into his back.

With Washington lying against the front of the car, the police officer tells him to 'stay still'

The officer is heard screaming on the video: 'Shut the f**k up' as Washington whimpered and cried in pain as he repeatedly said: 'Oh my God.'

Elmore asked: 'Do you want it again?' He cried as she said: 'Shut your b***h a** up.' 

'Washington cried, begged and pleaded for Elmore to stop,' Maxwell said in the release. 

Maxwell said his client was handcuffed for the entire encounter, did not resist and posed no threat to the officer. 

'He did everything right and was still the victim of cruel, hateful, excessive and unlawful use of force,' his lawyer said. 

After Washington was arrested, he was taken to Pickens County jail and charged with trafficking fentanyl, obstructing governmental operations, resisting arrest, possession of marijuana and being an ex-felon in possession of a firearm.

The fentanyl trafficking charge and its accompanying $500,000 bond were later dropped after 'further testing' showed the substance in his possession was not the opioid drug. 

Washington spoke out about the incident in a recent interview with ABC where he detailed what happened during his arrest

Maxwell said his clients case is still be investigated and that charges against Washington 'remain pending,' but he expects the remining charges to be dismissed

In his interview, Washington said even though he was being targeted during the encounter, he was more concerned about his brother and worried something might happen to him. 

He also believes he would still be in jail if his brother did not record the moment.  

'I just want justice, honestly, for her to get what's coming to her on the legal side,' he said. 

The State Bureau of Investigation and the Federal Bureau of Investigation are investigating the case.
