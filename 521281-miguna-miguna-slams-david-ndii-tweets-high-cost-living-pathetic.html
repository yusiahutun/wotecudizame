Miguna Miguna Slams David Ndii's Insensitive Tweets on High Cost of Living: &quot;This Is Pathetic&quot;

Publish date: 2024-07-22
  • Lawyer Miguna Miguna accused President William Ruto's senior economic advisor, David Ndii of failing to offer practical advice to the government
  • Miguna said Kenyans were sick and tired of hearing the same old failed economic policies and deserved fresh, concrete solutions.
  • The outspoken lawyer said Ndii should consider resigning if he cannot advise the president accordingly

Nairobi - Lawyer Miguna Miguna has heavily criticised President William Ruto's senior economic advisor, David Ndii, over his remarks on the state of the economy.

What David Ndii said about the cost of living

On Friday, September 15, Ndii, the Council of Economic Advisers Chair, appeared to suggest that Kenya Kwanza's economic recovery strategy is mere guesswork.

While answering a question from a Kenya who wanted to know if the economic situation in the country would get better or if it would get progressively worse, Ndii casually replied that it might be worse or not.

"Maybe better, maybe worse, who knows. We do what we think can work if it doesn't too bad. Doctors lose patients all the time despite their best efforts," Ndii said on X, formerly Twitter.

Reacting to Ndii's tweets, Miguna said it was pathetic, especially from a man trusted by the president to offer solid economic strategies.

According to the outspoken lawyer, the government needs to have strategic plans and programs on how to solve Kenyan problems both in the short and long term.

"This is pathetic for a chair of economic advisors at the presidency. Unless we are speaking about voodoo economics, the President’s economic team must have strategies, plans and programs aimed at solving Kenya’s intractable problems - both short term and long term," Miguna said.

Should David Ndii resign as Ruto's advisor?

Miguna said Ndii should consider resigning if he lacks practical plans to help the president run the economy.

"But for the Chair of the Council of Economic Advisors to premise his economic strategies and plans for the country on the basis of speculation and “it might work or fail, who knows?” is to take Kenyans for fools," he added.

Miguna said Kenyans are sick and tired of hearing the same old failed economic policies and deserve fresh, concrete solutions.

"Kenyans are sick and tired of hearing the same old failed economic policies for Africa by the Bretton Woods institutions.You occupy senior public positions to come up with fresh and concrete solutions to Kenyans’ problems, not to speculate, engage in voodoo economics, or smugly dismiss citizens’ concerns," Miguna stated.

Ndii warns Kenyans against trusting Ruto government

As reported earlier, Ndii caused turmoil on Twitter after appearing to disown the government for which he strategists.

Ndii barraged Kenyans with lamentations, suggesting nothing good would come out of the government in the near future.

An abrasive Ndii essentially dismissed any possibility of the current administration turning around the economy as its owners had promised during the campaigns.

According to him, the mess was created over a decade ago, and it would not be cleared without compatriots bearing the brunt.
