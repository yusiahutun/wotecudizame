List of all Swahili radio stations in Kenya and their frequencies

Publish date: 2024-01-27

As you know, one’s mother tongue is their first language, and for Kenya, Swahili is her first language. Swahili as the native language is spoken widely across the country and finds its heart in Kenya’s coastal region. It has been incorporated into various sectors as a national language, including the entertainment industry. In addition, there are several Swahili radio stations in Kenya that people can listen to wherever they are as they keep up the daily hustles.

To maintain its ethnic language, Kenyan schools teach Kiswahili and offer specialized courses at higher education levels for mastery of the language. Foreigners also embrace the language when they tour the country by quickly learning to blend well with Kenyan citizens.

Swahili radio stations in Kenya

If you don’t understand English or you’re a patriotic Kenyan who believes in speaking your native language, you haven’t been completely locked out of the radio listeners group. As much as English radio stations are the most popular and there is a high rise of vernacular radio stations, there is something in place for you, Swahili radio stations.

There are several stations in Kenya distributed across various counties. Therefore, you can listen to Swahili radio stations in Nairobi and other parts of the country. Additionally, you can tune in from your computer or smartphone and listen to the Swahili radio online. Here’s a list of radio stations in Kenya that present in Swahili.

1. Radio Citizen - Chemi Chemi Ya Ukweli

Radio Citizen is the number one Swahili radio station in Kenya. With coverage in most of the major towns in Kenya, it has the biggest audience. Radio Citizen was rated the first radio station since it has the highest listenership among other radio stations.

The broadcast targets the ordinary citizen and offers variety in the news, engaging talk shows, African tunes, and games. It's famous for shows such as Roga Roga, Yaliyotendeka, Jambo Kenya, Chapa Kazi, and Drive on. Its frequencies across the country include:

At the heart of the Kiswahili language lies a Swahili radio station loved by the coastal people. Pwani FM has the most fluent Kiswahili radio presenters, so if you’re looking to listen to a station that will sharpen and improve your Swahili, this is the one.

7. Radio Safari- Safiri Nasi

If you are in the environs of Kitale, Trans Nzoia county, you can’t afford to miss listening to this Swahili radio station. It has established itself to be a leading, reliable, fair and fast-growing station since 2018. You can tune in on 89.7 FM and online on radiosafari.co.ke.

8. Radio Simba- Sauti Ya Jamii

The western region is also well represented with this Swahili radio station based in Bungoma, targeting Western, Rift Valley, and Nyanza regions. The station broadcasts updates, information, music, news, entertainment and educational matters. You can tune in to 91.3 FM or visit their website to listen live.

9. Sema FM- Sauti Ya Vijanaa

The Swahili radio station is based in Webuye and has presenters passionate about entertaining their listeners in the best possible way. Sema FM is broadcasted on 98.3 FM and is also listened to live online.

Even though English radio stations have populated the airwaves, there are Swahili radio stations you can listen to as you go about your day. They are also good if you’re looking to switch between English and Swahili stations. The stations are also available online; therefore, you can listen to them anywhere and at any time.
