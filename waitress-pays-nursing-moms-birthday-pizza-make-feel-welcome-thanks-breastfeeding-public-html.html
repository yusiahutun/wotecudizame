Kind-hearted waitress pays for mom's birthday pizza to make her feel welcome and THANKS her for breastfeeding in public

The Internet abounds with horror stories about women who have been bullied for breastfeeding in public, which is why a nursing mom from Iowa was stunned when a waitress at a pizza parlor surprised her by paying for her dinner.

Ankeny resident Jackie Johnson-Smith, her husband and their three young children, ages 4, 3, and 12 months, went out to Fong's Pizza in Des Moines Sunday to celebrate the woman's 33rd birthday.

A few minutes into the dinner, the couple's youngest son, Stellen, became fussy.

Birthday present: Jackie Johnson-Smith was celebrating her 33rd birthday with her husband and three children (pictured) at a De Moines pizza shop when she got an unexpected surprise from a waitress

Sweet gesture: Waitress Bodi Kinney (left) handed Johnson-Smith's husband a receipt (right), on which she wrote that she had paid for one of the family's pizzas and asked him to thank his wife for breastfeeding

Mrs Johnson-Smith took the boy in her arms, placed a nursing cover over her front and reluctantly began breastfeeding him.

In an interview with Yahoo! Shine later, the stay-at-home mom explained that on previous occasions, she has been subjected to criticism for nursing in public, so she generally tries to avoid the practice for fear of being ostracized.

Mother's touch: Baby Stellen became fussy at the table, so his mother decided to calm him down by nursing him

While feeding Stellen, the mother of three noticed waitress Bodi Kinney, 33, glancing her way, and she became concerned that the woman was judging her.

‘I instantly felt panicked. Like, “What is she thinking? Is she not wanting me to do it here?’” the mother recalled to the local station KCCI.

Being a mom herself, however, Kinney made an instant connection with Mrs Johnson-Smith and decided to put her at ease by partially covering the family’s tab.

Mrs Johnson-Smith, who at one point went outside the restaurant to finish nursing her son in the car, was speechless when her husband handed her the receipt.

On the bill, the waitress left a hand-written message that read: ‘I bought one of your pizzas. Please thank your wife for breastfeeding!’

‘I read it several times, and tears started welling in my eyes,’ Johnson-Smith said. ‘And I thought, I can’t believe this just happened.’

The woman was so touched by the gesture from a complete stranger that she photographed the bill with Kinney's missive and posted it on her Facebook page along with a comment explaining the situation.

Family friendly: The incident took place at Fong's Pizza in downtown De Moines on Sunday

‘I have breastfed three children... I have breastfed them in countless of places both pleasant and unpleasant, discreetly and out in the open. I have gotten many looks and stares, but tonight erases any negativity I have ever received,’ she wrote.

The 33-year-old added, ‘Although I don't need a pat on the back for feeding my child, it sure felt amazing. It is amazing how we women can make each other feel when we empower each other.’

The story clearly struck a nerve with moms and families across the nation and around the world, quickly going viral online. By Thursday night, the image of the receipt has been shared more than 2,500 times.

Special bond: Johnson-Smith (left) later wrote a letter to Kinney to thank her for her act of kindness

In an interview with KCCI, Kinney explained that she just wanted to do something for a fellow mother.

Although Johnson-Smith was too overcome with emotion to personally thank Kinney on the night of the dinner, she later wrote a letter thanking her for her touching gesture.
