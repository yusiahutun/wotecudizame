The 10 WORST celebrity hair extension disasters revealed

Publish date: 2024-01-28

When former Baywatch babe Pamela Anderson showcased a Sixties-style bouffant recently, her hairstyle wasn't quite as glamorous as she'd hoped.

Thanks to ill-fitting extensions, the 47-year-old had visible bonds showing and the fake locks did not blend seamlessly with her own tresses.

She's not the first celebrity to suffer from a barnet malfunction thanks to hair extensions. From Paris Hilton to Britney Spears, there are a plenty of examples of how the popular beauty product has left the hair of celebrities looking lacklustre rather than luscious.

Bad bouffant: Pamela Anderson was seen sporting visible bonds at a gala event last week

Even stylish celebrities like Beyoncé and Victoria Beckham have some extension nightmares they would rather forget. 

But now online hair and beauty retailer Hairtrade.com has compiled a list of the ten worst offenders to show people how to avoid a bad hair day.

A spokesperson for the brand said: 'We've all got photos that we look back on and say, "what was I thinking?" So it's reassuring to know that no matter how perfect these celebs seem, they've had their share of hair care faux pas too.

'Hair extensions may seem like a quick fix to get the hair you want but unless they are applied with care it can lead to some sticky situations and embarrassing photos. Everyone wants long, thick, luscious hair and extensions are a great way of achieving this, you just need to take time and find the right ones for you.'

Here's the ten worst celebrity hair extension disasters...

1. Britney Spears

Recently Britney has been looking great in preparation for her Piece of Me tour in Las Vegas but no one can forget those dodgy hair extensions of the mid 2000s.

Ms Spears is definitely not that innocent of wearing bad hair extensions. Nothing can beat the nest of tendrils and visible scalp nightmares she's had in the past. 

Britney's 'nest of tendrils' seen while shopping in California in 2010 has to be the worst extensions disaster

If you are considering getting hair extensions, here's the prime example of what not to do.

The experts at Kapello Hair extensions say Britney's hair has got this way because she hasn't taken proper care of it.

National account manager Simon, who's worked with hair extensions for 25 years, says: 'It is vital that you take care of your hair extensions and treat them properly, you wouldn't put a cashmere sweater in the washing machine, or buy a new car and never have it serviced. 

'Britney should never have let her extensions get in this condition.'

Britney looks fab these days but no one can forget those dodgy hair extensions of 2004 (r) and 2008 (l)

2. Lindsay Lohan

The actress' dodgy two-toned fiasco is the best advertisement for the importance of colour-matching your hair extensions to blend with your natural colour.

Kapello's Simon explains: 'It is vital to have a consultation with your extensions technician. Lilo's extensions certainly do show the importance of closely colour-matching your hair. 

'However the match does not have to be 100 per cent exact. A shade either side of your natural colour can easily blend, especially if you curl your locks.'

Lindsay Lohan's two-toned fiascos seen in 2012, left, and 2011 right, show the importance of colour-matching

3. Pamela Anderson

Pamela's latest look harks back to her rocky history with hair extensions. 

Visible bonds were protruding from the top of her head earlier this month as she tried to go for a signature voluminous look with the help of a few extra strands.

Pamela Anderson arrives at a charity gala earlier this month sporting dodgy extensions

It's not the first time Pamela has had a bad hair day, as her matted extensions in 2005 prove

With literally hair-raising roots and a clearly visible scalp, the model turned actress has seen more than one such bad hair day. 

'In order to be invisible, extensions shouldn't be placed too high on the head,' says Simon.

'If you wear your hair 'up' they shouldn't be placed too low, as your natural hair needs to cover the attachment of the extensions.'

4. Paris Hilton

She may be a multi-millionaire but the heiress' straggly hair extensions during the early 2000s certainly didn't show it.

We much prefer the darker locks the socialite has been rocking more recently and it seems she's finally learnt a thing or two about how to wear hair extensions.

Heiress Paris rocked straggly hair extensions along with sister Nicky back in 2003

Simon suggests Paris' straggly look may be down to using single drawn extensions.

'There is an industry term called double-drawn,' he says. 'This is when extensions manufacturers remove the shorter hairs, which make up the individual extension. If the extension is only 'single-drawn' the ends will look thin and wispy.' 

5. Victoria Beckham

She's considered one of the most stylish women on the planet but Posh Spice doesn't always get it right when she wears hair extensions.

She's been guilty of having bonds too close to the hairline and extensions that aren't the right colour match.

Simon advises: 'Always follow the two finger rule - do not put extensions within the thickness of two fingers from the hairline.'

Bonds too close to the hairline can make extensions look even more obvious, as Victoria Beckham demonstrated in 2005 at the Pride of Britain Awards, left, and presenting her fashion collection in 2010, right

6. Katie Price

Katie Price has been taking better care of her hair more recently but the toll of her extensions on her scalp was obvious when she was wearing them everyday.

The experts recommend that if you are wearing synthetic hair extensions as opposed to bonded human hair ones - like Katie did in 2011 - you should avoid wearing them everyday and instead save for special occasions.

'Although there are synthetic hair products on the market, which are very affordable, and some that can be styled with heat tools, synthetic hair often looks too shiny,' muses Simon.

'Synthetic extensions won't last as long as real hair, so it may be a false economy.'

If you are wearing synthetic hair extensions, try to avoid wearing them everyday like Katie in 2011 as they will end up looking unnatural and too shiny 

7. Taylor Momsen

If you are using hair extensions to make your hair look longer, don't go overboard like Gossip Girl star Taylor Momsen. Stick to a more natural and realistic length.

'Here we have another example of what appear to be single drawn extensions,' says Simon.

'For example, 18 inch extensions will be made up of various lengths up to 18 inch long. So always buy the best extensions you can afford and look after them. 

The length of the Gossip Girl star's hair extensions was astronomical in 2010

8. Nicole Richie

Back in the early 2000's Nicole Richie sported some questionable dip dye hair extensions.

If you want have two tones in your hair extensions, pick colours that blend more naturally together to avoid stark styling.

'Extensions are a great way to have colour without the commitment of having your regrowth re-coloured. Unless you are going for a particularly edgy look, remember that certain colour combinations will look much better than others,' advises Simon.

Nicole Richie got the colour combination wrong when she wore these hair extensions in 2003, left at the Billboard Music Awards, and right on The Tonight Show   

9. Christina Aguilera

The Voice judge has often opted for unusual hairstyles but her permed hair extensions with straight pink streaks worn in 2001 have got to be one of her worst.

If you are wearing hair extensions, make sure to keep your styling consistent across every strand otherwise it will show in photos.

Christina's permed hair extensions with straight pink streaks from 2001 have got to be one of her worst looks

10. Beyoncé

Even the self-proclaimed Flawless Queen B doesn't always get it right. Her extra-long braided hair extensions from May last year looked straggly and impractical.

We much prefer the singer's two-toned bob look with the help of some more natural-looking hair extensions. 

Simon from Kapello says: 'The hair extensions technician's number one mission is to create a natural look - they don't want people to see their work. 

'Make sure that your hair style looks realistic. Extremely long hair may get you noticed - but not for the right reasons!'

Queen B's extra long braided hair extensions worn in 2014 look straggly and impractical 

Kapello's top five tips for hair extensions that stay lush for longer

1) Use high quality products to wash and style your hair extensions, and use a hair treatment every month to restore their condition

2) Use heat protective spray, and try not to use too much heat when styling them

3) Leave your extensions to air dry after washing, try not to brush the hair when wet

4) If you wear clip-in extensions take them out before you go to bed, and keep them in a dry environment

5) Take your clip-in hair extensions with you to your hairdressers. Clip-in extensions can benefit from being cut to suit your face shape just like your natural hair
