15 Disney birds characters ranked according to popularity

Publish date: 2024-05-07

Birds have made their way into some of the best Disney movies. Disney has quite a few feathers in its cap, including ducks, parrots, hornbills, eagles, and even chicken. Most of the birds have appeared as fictional characters in animated movies. But which are the most popular Disney birds characters?

Since the beginning of cinema, animal species have played an important role in films. Birds are one of the animal species that have had the most appearances in cinema throughout history. The use of birds in Disney movies goes back as far as 1930. Since then, their appearance in movies has increased and shows no sign of stopping.

Disney birds characters

When it comes to Disney birds' names, a few stand out from the rest. Besides Donald Duck and Orange Bird, there are many other Disney bird characters. Here are the most popular bird characters that have been featured in Disney movies.

1. Owl - Winnie the Pooh

First on the list of the most famous Disney bird characters is the Owl. This is an anthropomorphic owl renowned for his appearance in Winnie the Pooh and the Honey Tree. He is considered a know-it-all, but his friends always seek his advice. His character is based on the stereotype of the wise old owl. Unfortunately, he often misses the mark.

2. Iago - Aladdin (1992)

This is the most famous parrot in Disney. Iago is sarcastic, loud-mouthed, and short-tempered. The parrot is the secondary antagonist of the animated feature film. He serves as an aid to nasty power-hungry Grand Vizier Jafar. The mischievous little parrot is renowned for his love for sarcasm and the finer things in life. He does not shy away from speaking his mind.

3. Donald Duck - Fantasia 2000 (1999)

Donald Duck is one of the most famous Disney movie birds. He plays the role of Noah's assistant in Disney's Fantasia 2000. Donald is mandated with the responsibility of gathering the animals to the Ark, and he reunites with Daisy in the process. His character is voiced by Tony Anselmo.

4. Pico the Toucan- Encanto (2022)

Pico is a minor character in Disney's 60th animated feature film Encanto. He is a cheeky keel-billed toucan who is a friend of Antonio Madrigal. Pico was responsible for inviting the rest of Encanto's wildlife into the Madrigal family. His character is voiced by Alan Tudyk.

5. Zazu - The Lion King (1994)

You know that friend who you can always count on to talk some sense into you. Zazu is that type of friend. He is a major character in Disney's 1994 animated feature film, The Lion King. He is an uptight, red-billed hornbill. The male hornbill currently serves as the majordomo of the Pride Lands. Zazu has also appeared a few times in Simba Timon and Pumbaa.

6. Kevin - Up (2009)

Kevin is one of the most famous female bird characters. She is the female tritagonist in the animated feature film, Up. Kevin is a giant, South American bird and the main target of Charles Muntz. She is based on the male Himalayan monal pheasant. Kevin doesn't have any speaking lines in the movie, but she is renowned for her vibrant personality.

7. Scuttle - The Little Mermaid

This is one of the most famous white birds in Disney. Scuttle is a supporting character in the animated film. He is an eccentric seagull who lives off the coast of Prince Eric's kingdom. In the 1989 movie, the clumsy bird considered himself an expert on humans and their inventions. Scuttle serves as an advisor to the film's protagonist, Ariel.

8. Lady Kluck - Robin Hood (1973)

Lady Kluck is a famous blue bird in Disney. She plays the role of a supporting character in the animated feature film Robin Hood. Lady Kluck is the obese and muscular lady-in-waiting of Maid Marian. Her character was voiced by Carole Shelley.

9. Becky - Finding Dory (2016)

Becky is a supporting character in the 2016 Disney animated film Finding Dory. She appears as a small bird with black and white-speckled feathers and white under feathers. Becky is the loon that Dory and her friends encounter at the Marine Life Institute. She helps them navigate the institute and aids in their successful reunion with Dory's parents.

10. Ace Cluck - Chicken Little (2005)

Ace Cluck is the titular protagonist in Chicken Little. He is the son of Buck and Chloe. Ace Cluck has white feathers, a red comb, and dark brown eyebrows. He appears as a young and diminutive rooster and has a reputation for being called crazy. Ace Cluck tends to worry about everything and can be impulsive and quick to jump to conclusions.

11. Trigger and Nutsy - Robin Hood (1973)

Trigger and Nutsy are two vultures from the film Robin Hood. They are the supporting antagonists in the animated feature film. Nutsy wears a hood, while Trigger wears a helmet and carries a crossbow and a quiver of arrows. They have contrasting personalities. Trigger is smarter than Nutsy. On the other hand, Nutsy is extremely dimwitted as he acts impulsively.

12. Marahute - The Rescuers Down Under (1990)

Marahute is a giant golden mother eagle who had the baby eggs from Disney's Rescuers Down Under. She is portrayed as a very kind and gentle giant eagle. Her character is voiced by Frank Welker.

13. Sam Eagle - The Muppets

Sam Eagle is a Muppet character best known for his ultra-patriotism and disciplined manner of being. Sam's overtly patriotic spirit distinguishes him from the rest of the Muppet cast. He first appeared on The Muppet Show and has appeared in every Muppet movie to date.

14. Scrooge McDuck- Ducktales (2017)

Scrooge is a cartoon character created in 1947 for Disney by Carl Barks. He is the main protagonist of the 2017 DuckTales reboot series. The iconic Disney character is the richest duck in Duckburg, a city popularized by the animated series. Like his nephew, Donald Duck, he has a yellow-orange bill, legs, and feet. His character was voiced by David Tennant.

15. Hei Hei - Moana (2016)

If you are looking for dumb birds in Disney movies, Heihei should be at the top of your list. In Disney's Moana, Heihei, is the dumbest chicken. He is a supporting character in the 2016 animated feature film. The rooster unintentionally joins Moana and Maui on their journey across the ocean. His character was voiced by Alan Tudyk.
