Precious is a 2009 American drama movie based on the novel Push by Sapphire. The movie is co-produced and directed by renowned director, producer and screenwriter Lee Daniels. It stars talented actors and actresses such as Mariah Carey, Mo’Nique, Gabourey Sidibe and Paula Patton.

Is the Precious movie based on a true story?

No, it is not a true story. The film is based on the experiences of Sapphire, an African American novelist and poet working as a teacher in Harlem in the 1980s. Precious is not about one specific woman but rather an amalgam of many young women.

What is the story behind the movie Precious?

The film is based on a book titled Precious by Sapphire. Initially, the book was titled Push but was later re-titled after the release of the 2009 movie. The film is about a 16-years old illiterate, obese, HIV-positive and pregnant girl.

She lives her life in the dark, feeling ugly and stupid. However, later on, she grows into a young girl with poise and self-believe thanks to the dedication and compassion of a few women around her, such as the literacy teacher in Harlem and the social worker.

Whose life is the movie Precious based on? The author drew her inspiration from her childhood experiences. Her father, a sergeant in the US Army, sexually abused her when she was eight years old. On the other hand, her mother, a professional nurse, abandoned her at 13.

Precious plot

The film narrates the story of Claireece Precious Jones, a 16-year-old girl from the Harlem neighbourhood in New York City. She lives with her unemployed mother, Mary. Her mother constantly subjects her to verbal and physical abuse while her now-absent father, Carl, took her by force her resulting in two pregnancies.

Her firstborn child, Mongo, a short form for Mongoloid, suffers from Down Syndrome and lives with Precious’ grandmother. Mary forces the family to pretend they live with Mongo to receive extra money from the government.

Mr Lichtenstein, Precious' high school principal, discovers she is carrying a second pregnancy. So she arranges for Jones to enrol at an alternative school program called Each One Teach One. The principal hopes this will give Precious a chance to change her life. To escape her daily traumatic life, she escapes into daydreaming that she is loved and appreciated.
