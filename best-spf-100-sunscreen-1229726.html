What Does the SPF 100 Sunscreen do?

Sunscreen provides your skin with a physical layer of protection that absorbs the harmful UV rays and prevents your skin cells from damaging and from issues like from sunburn and tanning. The SPF level in sunscreen is a measurement to gauge how much of the UV rays will be absorbed or reflected away from your skin. Which means, a sunscreen SPF 100 will ward off upto 99.9% of the harmful rays whereas the lower SPF are effective only against 97%.

    How to Choose a Sunscreen With SPF 100?

    Skin type: Before you try any product that goes on your body, it is absolutely necessary to understand if your skin is sensitive or not. If you have a product that has a strong formula and is not skin-friendly, your skin could have bad allergic reactions. So, sunscreen like La Roche-Posay SPF 100 provides adequate protection for all skin types.

    Ingredients: You need to be on the lookout for ingredients that are harmful in general like oxybenzone, octinoxate, or alcohol which does more damage than good for your skin. But active ingredients like extracts from fruits, leaves, and other natural substances are a great source of antioxidant benefits to delay any early aging signs.

    Formula: Try and choose a formula that retains your freedom of movement when applied. A formula that is smooth and light does not dry up quickly and does not feel heavy when applied. Using a formula that dries up quickly might also prove to be difficult in washing them off. Something like Banana Boat Sport Ultra SPF 100 is a great choice for that.

    Water-resistance: If you are going to spend a lot of time in water, then you should choose water-resistant sunscreens. Usually, sunscreens allow water-resistance for upto 80 minutes. If you need longer protection, you’ll need to reapply the sunscreen.
