What did Jon Stewart get up to during his The Daily Show hiatus? Returning just in time for the 2024

Publish date: 2024-09-05

Even throughout his hiatus from the show though, he remained one of America’s most influential and thought-provoking voices. It was Stewart who created a new niche of political satire in US culture, and he has been instrumental in helping and influencing the careers of major comedians today including Trevor Noah, John Oliver and Stephen Colbert.

He also advocated for 9/11 first responders and, in 2019, was awarded with the New York City Bronze Medallion for his efforts around the September 11th Victim Compensation Fund Act.

So what has he been up to since 2015? Let’s take a look ...

The Daily Show phenomenon

Stewart debuted as the host of The Daily Show on January 11, 1999. He was awkward in front of the cameras while speaking to his first guest, Michael J. Fox, as he tried to adjust to the previous format of the show. But then he found his voice, focused on what he was good at and took control. From 1999 to 2015, Stewart was one of America’s foremost social voices.

The Daily Show with Jon Stewart was the recipient of at least 60 awards and nominations, illustrating the widespread acknowledgement of his talents. In an interview with Vanity Fair, he said that his goal when he started was simply to have fun.

“Success for me would’ve been feeling like I figured it out. That I got to express the things I wanted to,” he said. “It was never ‘I want this to be a cultural touchstone, but only for a very small portion of America.’ And I was hoping to stay on TV longer than nine months this time.”

Stewart’s voice returns

In 2021, several years after his departure from The Daily Show, he debuted The Problem with Jon Stewart, a talk series on Apple TV+. The move was seen as a big coup for Apple and was part of a multi-year first look deal that Stewart signed. Season one aired episodes every two weeks, while season two aired weekly. The 60-minute show dealt with one topic per episode.

The controversial end

It was widely reported that The Problem with Jon Stewart would not get a third season. Although initial reports claimed the split between Apple and Stewart was amicable, The New York Times stated that Stewart and his bosses clashed over “creative differences”. Particular issues that caused tension included AI and China, per the publication.

According to The Guardian, US lawmakers asked Apple to explain why Stewart’s show ended, with concerns being raised that it was because of China. In a letter to Apple boss Tim Cook, a House committee wrote, “While companies have the right to determine what content is appropriate for their streaming service, the coercive tactics of a foreign power should not be directly or indirectly influencing these determinations.”

The short-lived animation venture

In 2015, it was announced that Stewart would collaborate with HBO to create short-form animation ventures designed for various platforms, but the project was eventually cancelled. HBO said at the time that it would continue to look at other projects with the comedian.

The Trevor Noah connection

South African comedian Trevor Noah took over as host of The Daily Show after Stewart left. Stewart and Noah have spoken highly of each other over the years, with Stewart acknowledging how Noah did better than him in some ways. Referring to Noah being more engaged in conversations on social media platforms, Stewart said: “For Trevor, it’s a part of him. It flows from him naturally … It makes it better. The show is better.”

In January, The Daily Show with Trevor Noah won an Emmy for outstanding talk series. Noah, who has since left the show, thanked Stewart for his contribution to his career.

“Honestly, the person I always thank because he’s a crazy genius for thinking of it, Jon Stewart,” he said. “Wherever you are my friend, thank you for calling me up and asking me to come and join you on this crazy journey.”
