Seven retro Cadbury chocolate bars that need to be brought back immediately

Publish date: 2024-04-15

Cadbury is celebrating its 200th anniversary.

And to mark the big occasion, the British confectioner announced seven of its famous Dairy Milk designs would be returning to our shelves.

For limited time, customers can get hold of retro packaging that was used as long ago as 1915. But we kind of wish some discontinued bars would be back too.

As Cadbury marks two centuries in existence, we're here to plead with the company to start making these products again. Here are seven iconic chocolates we'd love to see back on British shelves...

Milk Tray Bar – 1970s

Nothing screams single like getting a whole box of Milk Tray choccies and devouring them alone on the sofa. And trying not to fantasise about the Milk Tray man.

So we'd quite like the Milk Tray bars to come back, please Cadbury. Especially if they'd still cost 10p.

The bars were just the right size to enjoy solo and combined all our favourite flavours too. We're talking Strawberry cream, fudge and Turkish delight all wrapped into one super-bar.

Cadbury Taz – 1994 to late nineties

Why do smug Freddo frogs get all the glory, eh? The little bleeders have been a firm favourite with Brits for decades.

And how do they repay us? By rising in price by a sickening amount as the years go by.

They used to cost 10p in the noughties – but they're around 25p nowadays. That's a whopping 150% increase.

It's time to take a stand and bring back Taz bars instead. The Looney Toons character tastes just as carameley and delicious – and it's not holding us hostage.

Snowflake / Flake Snow – 2000-2008

What's better than a Dream bar and a Flake? A SNOWFLAKE, of course.

The combination of flaked white chocolate inside a smooth milk chocolate coating was unparalleled. Think a Twirl with extra accessories.

Even though Cadbury rebranded Snowflake to Flake Snow in a bid to up sales in 2003, it was sadly discontinued five years later due to lack of demand. You have no one to blame but yourselves.

Dairy Milk Spira – 1980s-2005

Want a bit of texture with your Dairy Milk? Back in the day, you could've reached for a Spira bar.

They had the same wonderfully creamy flavour with extra pizazz due to the shape. The hollow twisted spirals had a very satisfying snap when you bit into them too.

Our mouths are watering at the thought. We're sorry Spira, we should have treated you better.

Fuse – 1996-2006

If you have cereal in a chocolate bar, does it count as breakfast? It's a question we ask ourselves when we look back at Fuse bars.

The Cadbury creation consisted of nuts, raisins, fudge and cereal pieces. It was definitely unique, and we love that.

They still sell it in India, which some may think is worth the long haul journey.

Time Out – 1992 to 2016

Sometimes, Nestle's Kit Kat just doesn't cut it. So why not bring back Time Out?

The "wafer break with a layer of Flake" contained two wafers joined by ganache. It was then covered in a coating of Dairy Milk chocolate that we all know and love.

They do have Cadbury Time Out Wafers nowadays, but these don't quite hit the same spot. Just our opinion.

Wispa Mint – 1995-2003

Mint chocolate is a classic combination – so we're shocked these weren't more popular when they launched in 1995.

Cadbury made the aerated choccies to challenge Nestle's Aero, but the herby variety failed to resonate with the public and it was discontinued in 2003.

You can still get regular Wispa bars, and mini ones in your boxes of Heroes – but now we want mint back!
