Mariusz Pudzianowski ready for Eddie Hall or Thor Bjornsson

Publish date: 2024-08-05

Mariusz Pudzianowski has already challenged Hafthor Bjornsson and Eddie Hall to a brutal MMA showdown following their impressive outing in the boxing ring on Saturday (March 19).

The five-time World's Strongest Man, who turned to fighting after his final triumph in 2009, has been followed in winning that trophy by both Bjornsson and Hall, who squared off after years of resentment.

When speaking to Daily Star Sport last year about a potential fight against the winner of that bout, Pudzianowski admitted he was keen - provided they met in an arena that was suited to the skill set he has formed over the last 13 years.

He said: "I'm not considering moving to boxing. I have already focused on MMA and there will be no new challenge for me. MMA is probably the last challenge for me.

"But of course, with anyone - with Eddie or Bjornsson, I would enter the cage on the principles of MMA!"

Now 45, Pudzianowski began his professional MMA career back in 2009, has now secured blistering TKO victories in each of his last four bouts following 12 wins from his first 19 outings.

Pudzianowski wrote on social media last year that negotiations between him and Bjornsson had taken place over a potential meeting after the Hall fight was delayed.

He tagged Bjornsson and said: "It's getting closer to finalising the contract!"

Adding: "When it comes to fruition, it will be a challenge for hell."

And concluding: "If he hits, he may drop me but he has to catch me."

Hall called out Pudzianowski last week while predicting a win over Bjornsson, but a fight could still go ahead should he agree to the terms of the Polish star.

Hall told Muscle and Health : "I’m not saying I wanna fight with pro boxers, but I think this sort of clash of the celebrities has really taken off.

"Mariusz Pudzianowski has been coming onto my channel and having met him, he seems like a nice guy.

"So it’d be good to do business. It could be another battle of the strongmen. Another clash of the title."
