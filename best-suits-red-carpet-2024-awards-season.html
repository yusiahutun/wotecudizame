1

Ayo Edebiri

Axelle/Bauer-Griffin//Getty Images

The Bear star looked oh so cool at the Critics Choice Awards on January 14 in a monochromatic white suit, composed of an oversize blazer over a loose T-shirt and straight-leg slacks, by the Row. She changed things up with her footwear, opting for black ballet flats, and finished it all off with round Oliver Peoples shades.

2

Phoebe Bridgers, Lucy Dacus, and Julien Baker

Axelle/Bauer-Griffin//Getty Images

Boygenius—one of the coolest trios in music—made the most iconic entrance at the Grammys on February 4 in matching ivory Thom Browne suits that featured an oily wood-grain pattern. They kept things crisp with white button-down shirts, inky-black ties, black ankle socks, and glossy dress shoes. The ensemble served as a perfect canvas for the pink flowers and red Artists4Ceasefire pins they wore on their lapels.

3

Billie Eilish

JC Olivera//Getty Images

The “What Was I Made For?” hitmaker looked sophisticated in a Chanel ensemble that put a Barbie spin on the office siren trend at the Oscars on March 10. The vintage-looking skirt suit included a white high-neck blouse under an inky-black blazer that featured glossy buttons and a flared-out hem. Eilish paired the top with a tweed black-and-white pencil skirt, high white socks, and shiny Mary Janes. Her small black-and-white top-handle purse was the perfect final old-timey touch.

4

Billie Eilish

Robert Smith//Getty Images

Hours later, for the Vanity Fair Oscars after-party, Eilish returned to her trademark oversize style in a boxy black pinstripe blazer and matching wide-leg pants with a large cuff at the hem, paired with a large white pinstripe button-down by Vaquera.

5

Meryl Streep

Axelle/Bauer-Griffin//Getty Images

The Devil Wears Prada star brought the shimmer to the Golden Globes on January 7 in a custom Valentino skirt suit, composed of a flowy white blouse with a tie front detail paired with a dazzling oversize black blazer and matching sweeping skirt—both of which were completely covered in glittery sequins. Diamond drop earrings, black platform heels, black cat-eye shades, and a silver-bead-bedazzled black clutch made the perfect accents.

6

America Ferrera

Unique Nicole//Getty Images

A classic black tuxedo is as timeless as a classic black gown, and the Barbie star made the former look so new at the Governors Awards on January 9. The custom Moschino ensemble consisted of an oversize suit jacket with dramatic, sharp lapels, flowy black pants with a slightly lighter cummerbund, a crisp white button-down shirt with inky-black buttons, and a large black bow tie. She added a feminine touch with pointed-toe stilettos.

7

Margot Robbie

Axelle/Bauer-Griffin//Getty Images

Does this count as a suit look? We think so. Robbie nodded to a vintage Barbie look in this deconstructed suit dress by Balmain at the Producers Guild Awards on February 25. The LBD featured a dramatic peplum waist, a satin opulent-white off-the-shoulder construction, and two matching sharp lapels along the bust. She kept things classic with black pointed-toe pumps, a small structured top-handle purse, and a gorgeous diamond necklace by Jacob & Co. jewelry.

8

Margot Robbie

Michael Buckner//Getty Images

Robbie Barbie-fied the suit once again in a Chanel ensemble at the Oscars Nominees Luncheon on February 12. Her look consisted of a cropped blazer that she wore open to reveal a matching bandeau top, paired with a matching tiered high-waisted miniskirt. All three pieces, as well as her small purse, were made of a creamy tweed material with hints of pastel pink and purple diamond-shaped stitching throughout. She finished it off with bone-white slingback heels with a black toe detail by Manolo Blahnik.

9

Ariana Greenblatt

Steve Granitz//Getty Images

The Barbie star put a sexy spin on classic tux staples at the Golden Globes on January 7. Her sultry look consisted of a crisp white button-up shirt that she wore unbuttoned to create a plunging neckline, tucked into ink-black trousers with a wide satin cummerbund, both by Yves Saint Laurent. She elevated the look with layers of diamond necklaces and stacks of diamond rings.

10

Jennifer Aniston

Axelle/Bauer-Griffin//Getty Images

The Friends alum made officewear look so posh at the Critics Choice Awards on January 14, in a tube top with a sweetheart neckline that was completely covered in soft black feathers, tucked into matching tailored, high-waisted slacks, both by Dolce & Gabbana. She added an ultra-long cummerbund detail that trailed at her side, classic pointed-toe pumps, and diamond drop earrings by De Beers.

11

Kourtney Kardashian

Neilson Barnard//Getty Images

There's only one thing cooler than wearing a monochromatic black suit on the red carpet: wearing matching monochromatic black suits with your hubby on the red carpet. That's exactly what the Poosh founder did at the Emmys on January 15, 2024, making a statement in an oversize blazer with satin lapels over a lacy bra and paired with wide-leg slacks and pointed-toe pumps. Travis Barker looked suave beside her, adding a black button-down, glossy bowtie, and shiny dress shoes to his look. The pair solidified their Best Dressed Couple status with black shades.

12

Greta Lee

Gilbert Flores//Getty Images

The Past Lives star made black look so cheery at the Critics Choice Awards on January 14, in a dazzling collared long-sleeved top with buttons down the front, tucked into matching high-waisted, straight-leg pants from Loewe’s Spring/Summer 2024 ready-to-wear collection. She rounded things off with strappy black heels and large diamond cuff earrings by Tiffany & Co.

13

Greta Gerwig

Christopher Polk//Getty Images

The Barbie director made a black suit look so daring at the Vanity Fair Oscars Party on March 10, by wearing her oversize blazer with satin lapels over absolutely nothing, paired with matching slacks and a black clutch—all pieces by Yves Saint Laurent. She kept things classic with black Aquazzura heels and, to make things more glamorous, added layers of diamond necklaces of various widths, lengths, and charms by Boucheron.

14

Bel Powley

Gilbert Flores//Getty Images

Here we are bending the definition of suit again, but this look is too gorgeous to not give it credit. The King of Staten Island star’s outfit said: Tuxedo, but make it a princess dress. Hailing from Chanel’s Spring/Summer 2023 couture collection, the look had Powley turning heads at the Critics Choice Awards on January 14. The dress that featured a white tweed bodice resembling a suit jacket with ornate marble buttons, tulle ¾-sleeves with wispy floral details throughout, and a matching bubble skirt. Keeping on theme, she added a gauzy black bow tie choker, black kitten heels with silver bead details along the toes, and diamond statement earrings.

15

Bella Ramsey

Axelle/Bauer-Griffin//Getty Images

The Last of Us star looked like a sweet breath of spring air at the Critics Choice Awards on January 14, in a Thom Browne suit. The ensemble consisted of a tailored jacket and pants in a pretty mint-green hue with illustrations of wispy white flowers throughout, paired with matching dress shoes, a soft-white button-down shirt, and a pistachio striped tie.

Celine Song

The Past Lives director elevated an oversize black skirt suit, composed of a large blazer and pencil skirt, with a glossy satin white button-down and unique rod detail across the skirt’s waist, all by Loewe, at the Golden Globes on January 7. She finished off her look with chunky black Mary Janes and pearl stud earrings by Simon G.

Stephanie Hsu

The Everything Everywhere All at Once star had some fun with her suit at the Vanity Fair Oscars party on March 10, by championing a controversial trend. Hsu brought the party in a satin beige Kate Spade ensemble composed of a large double-breasted jacket (which she wore open) and matching capris—yes, they’re back! Annette Bening
