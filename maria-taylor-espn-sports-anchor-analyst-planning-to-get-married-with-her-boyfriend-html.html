As a talented analyst and host for ESPN and The SEC Network, Maria Taylor has spellbound the sports world all this long; dealing with the assignments to cover football, volleyball and men’s and Women’s basketball based on college.

Characterized by her lanky height of 6 feet 2 inches (1.87 m), she credits her earlier indulgence in sports to the education she received. While in her school and college for the vast knowledge she possessed about the way the sporting world runs.

From Avid Player To Sportscaster

Maria was destined to go big in the sports field. However, she initially took the direct way of making the difference by playing herself.

Attending Centennial High School, she was introduced to the beauty of basketball and volleyball, and she became pretty inclined to it.

By the time she came of age for University, she had already clinched numbers of awards under her name. Including Fulton County Scholar-Athlete of the Year and three-time All-region Selection that got her sports scholarship at the University of Georgia in 2005.

Maria Taylor playing volleyball during her college days (Photo: espnfrontrow.com)

She also made it into the 2004 USA Volleyball Junior National A2 team. Before pursuing her media career in 2008, she was an avid sportswoman and was fourth all-time in total points with 2,020 points by the end of her career. 

She started her career as a regular in CSS’s volleyball broadcasts after graduation.

Since then, she has never looked back climbing stairs after stairs from sideline reporter on ESPN2 to a college analyst and football reporter on the SEC Network since 2014.

The sportscaster has had a quiet personal life to this date remaining away from publicizing her relationship status and others. Despite having the microphone glued to her all the time, it is ironical how her private details fail to be breached. 

Is Maria Taylor Married? Husband?

Fans are sure to get a shock from the news, but Maria Taylor is finally married. Keeping a low dating profile for the past half-decade, the analyst - host has finally revealed her relationship status with no detail left to spare.

Maria tied the knot with boyfriend turned husband Rodney Blackstock - the director of sports marketing at Q4 SPORTS. Surprisingly enough the duo met back in 2014. As the ESPN anchor was watching a game of the Charlotte Hornets, her eyes caught an attractive stranger in the arena.

It was love at first sight. The two ended up talking all night.
